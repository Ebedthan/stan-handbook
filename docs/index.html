Preface

This handbook teaches you to write statistical models directly in Stan, the probabilistic programming language behind many modern Bayesian workflows.

Who this book is for

You are a life scientist, agronomist, or ecologist who:

  • is comfortable fitting linear models and ANOVA in R,
  • has heard of Bayesian statistics but found it abstract,
  • wants to move beyond black-box software and understand what the model is actually doing.

No prior knowledge of Stan or Bayesian inference is assumed. Familiarity with R and lm() / aov() is sufficient to begin.

How to use this handbook

Each chapter introduces one model family, shows a complete Stan program, and walks through interpretation and diagnostics. Every code block is runnable, you can copy it into your own script and experiment.

The chapters in Part II (linear models and ANOVA) are the heart of the book. Parts III-IV build on them progressively. Part V gives you the habits that separate a competent Stan user from a self-sufficient one.
